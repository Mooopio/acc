The Accounting Cycle

6 steps — memorize them IN ORDER. They always come in the exam.

1

🔍 Identify Transactions & Events

Look at what happened — did money move? Did the company buy, sell, borrow, or pay? If yes → it must be recorded. Not every event is a transaction (e.g. signing a contract with no money exchange yet is NOT recorded).

2

📝 Recording in Journal (Journalizing)

Write every transaction in chronological order in the General Journal. Always: DEBIT first, then CREDIT indented below. Debits = Credits always.

3

📤 Posting to Ledger (Transferring)

Transfer each journal entry to its individual T-Account in the Ledger. The ledger shows the running balance of every account separately.

4

⚖️ Preparing Trial Balance

List ALL accounts with their closing balances. Total Debits MUST EQUAL Total Credits. If not equal → you made an error somewhere. This checks math, NOT correctness of entries.

5

📊 Preparing Financial Statements

Use the trial balance to prepare: Income Statement → Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet). Income Statement first because you need Net Income for the Balance Sheet.

6

🔒 Closing Temporary Accounts

Close Revenue, Expenses, Gains, Losses, Drawings to zero them out for the new period. Use "Income Summary" as a bridge account. Permanent accounts (Assets, Liabilities, Capital) are NEVER closed.

7

✅ After-Closing Trial Balance

Final check — only permanent accounts remain (Assets, Liabilities, Capital). Temporary accounts should all show zero balance. Confirms your closing entries were correct.

⚡ Quick Memory Trick:
"I Just Posted Tremendous Financial Closing After"

Identify → Journal → Post → Trial Balance → Financial Statements → Close → After-Closing Trial Balance

📝 The Journal

The diary of the business. Every transaction written CHRONOLOGICALLY. Debit always first.

⚡
The Golden Rule of Every Journal Entry:
Total Debits ALWAYS = Total Credits. If they don't match → STOP, find the error before moving on. This is non-negotiable in accounting.
⬆️

What DEBITS Do (DR)

  • Assets — INCREASE
  • Expenses — INCREASE
  • Losses — INCREASE
  • Drawings — INCREASE
  • Liabilities — DECREASE
  • Revenues — DECREASE
  • Capital — DECREASE
⬇️

What CREDITS Do (CR)

  • Liabilities — INCREASE
  • Revenues — INCREASE
  • Gains — INCREASE
  • Capital — INCREASE
  • Assets — DECREASE
  • Expenses — DECREASE
  • Drawings — DECREASE
🧠 DEAD CLIC Memory Trick:
Debit = Drawings, Expenses, Assets, Decrease-in-Liabilities
Credit = Capital, Liabilities, Income (Revenue), Credit-increases-these

⚖️ Trial Balance

A list of all account balances. Left = Debit, Right = Credit. Must be EQUAL.

✅

What Trial Balance PROVES

  • That total DR = total CR in your ledger
  • Math is correct in your postings
  • You're ready for financial statements
❌

What Trial Balance Does NOT Prove

  • That the correct accounts were used
  • That entries were complete
  • That amounts weren't entered in wrong accounts

🔒 Closing Entries

Reset temporary accounts to ZERO at end of period. 4 closing entries — always in this order.

Temporary Accounts = REVENUE + EXPENSES + GAINS + LOSSES + DRAWINGS
These all go to ZERO every period → permanent accounts keep their balance forever

🧠 Exam Tricks & Mnemonics

The shortcuts that separate A students from everyone else.

🏆 The Most Important Rule

DEAD CLIC — What Goes Where

Drawings, Expenses, Assets, Debit-normal = DEBIT side accounts

Capital, Liabilities, Income, Credit-normal = CREDIT side accounts

When these accounts increase → use their normal side. When they decrease → use the opposite side.

🔥 Exam Trap

Drawings ≠ Expense — Don't Confuse Them!

Drawings is the owner taking money out for personal use. It is NOT an expense on the Income Statement. It only appears on the Balance Sheet under Owner's Equity (reduces capital). Exams love to trick you on this.

Expense → reduces Net Income → on Income Statement
Drawings → reduces Capital directly → on Balance Sheet only

💛 Memorize This Equation

The Accounting Equation (Never Breaks)

Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity

Every single transaction keeps this equation balanced. If you're unsure about an entry, check — does it still balance the equation?

🔥 Another Classic Trap

Prepaid Insurance/Rent = ASSET, Not Expense

When you pay BEFORE receiving the benefit → it's a Prepaid (Asset). When the period passes and you've used the service → it becomes an Expense.

Example: Paid $1,000 for 1-year insurance → DR Prepaid Insurance $1,000 (Asset), NOT expense yet!
